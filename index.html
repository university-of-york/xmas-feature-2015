2015

The Year in Review

It's been a busy year at the University of York. New buildings have been built, graduands have become graduates and this year's new influx of students have descended on to campus.

Scroll down to see some of the statistics that have made up 2015 for us.

1 new Chancellor.

Professor Sir Malcolm Grant was inaugurated in October as the university's sixth Chancellor.

42 awards were made to PhD students thanks to alumni donations.

175 birdboxes on campus this year.

That's in addition to the 50 bat boxes, 200 ducks and hundreds of other geese, moorhens and coots.

1,803 books added to the library thanks to suggestions from students via the MoreBooks scheme.
8,817 tickets issued for this year's Festival of Ideas, making it the biggest yet!

Over 150 talks, performances and exhibitions held over two weeks.

12,778 plants planted across campus
56,103 emails sent in a single day this year by staff and students from their uni accounts.
Our scientists slowed light down to 176,700mph

4,248,031 timetabled hours in the academic year.
