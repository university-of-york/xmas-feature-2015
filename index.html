2015

The Year in Review

It's been another busy year for the University. Scroll down to see some of the key numbers that have been part of our 2015.

Scroll down
1 new Chancellor.

In October we inaugurated our sixth Chancellor, Professor Sir Malcolm Grant.

A new 2nd in command.

Professor Saul Tendler joined us as Deputy Vice-Chancellor and Provost in September.

3 faculties.

Our departments are now organised into faculties for the first time: Arts and Humanities, Sciences, and Social Sciences.

3.5 hour wait in the queue for the final Willow Disco.

Our students mourned the infamous York nightclub as it closed its doors for the last time, consigning communal prawn crackers to the dustbin of history.

7 Research Champions appointed to promote our interdisciplinary collaborations.

The Champions unveiled their plans for our new research themes as part of the Chancellor's Inauguration.

10th in the UK for Research Council income.

We secured £46m in Research Council funding in 2014/15, an 81% increase on the previous year!

24 plays performed in the Drama Barn.

Our students “broke a leg” in shows from Rent to Richard III.

We ranked 25th worldwide in Arts and Humanities

in the THE World University Rankings, placing us among some of the best known Ivy League schools in the US.

29 delegates from around the world gathered on campus to create the York Accord.

The York Accord is a set of principles for protection and recovery for higher education in areas affected by armed conflict.

45 working days were spent clearing goose poo from paths around campus.

A single goose can produce around 100 kilograms of waste in a year!

Viewers from 51 countries watched graduation online.

If you can't be here in person, you can still be part of the big day.

60 scholarships for Masters study were awarded to students from under-represented groups.

Each award was worth Â£10,000, removing one of the biggest barriers to postgraduate study.

100 articles have been contributed to The Conversation by our academics.

The Conversation is an independent source of news and opinion, written by experts from the academic community.

103rd in the QS World University Rankings.

Up 17 places since last year!

121 undergraduate bursaries

were funded by donations from alumni and friends - thank you all!

182 public lectures on campus

with almost 18,000 attendees. Just going to timetabled classes? You're missing out!

227.5 to 141.5 was the final score as York beat Lancaster in the 51st annual Roses tournament.

The sporting extravaganza was kicked off by England football manager Roy Hodgson.

#RosesAreWhite

242 of our students studied abroad

with help from the Centre for Global Programmes.

251 visiting students came to York

from 28 countries around the world. Welcome, bienvenue, wilkommen!

Over 350 employers visited campus to promote opportunities to our students,

all eager to net a York graduate for their business.

1,302 students joined the new York Graduate Research School.

Headed by Dean Tom Stoneham, the School nurtures our thriving community of research students.

1,803 books were added to the library

thanks to suggestions from students via the MoreBooks scheme.

1,916 new research publications

were added to the York Research Database by our academics.

2,569 alumni and friends made a gift to the University.

Thank you so much! You've all made a huge difference.

3,376 modules were taught.

From astrophysics to zooarchaeology, we've got it all!

5,585 students graduated

including 2,363 BAs and BScs, 1,630 Masters and 316 PhDs. Congratulations!

We advertised over 11,000 career development opportunities to students

including graduate schemes, part-time work and volunteering.

12,778 plants were planted across campus

by our green-fingered grounds staff.

14,717 people booked to attend Open Days

including one visitor who travelled 10,000+ miles from Australia - g'day!

15,353 students made up this year's student community.

We couldn't do it without you. Thanks for all your hard work and contributions to University life.

31,500 people attended York Festival of Ideas events.

170 free talks, performances and exhibitions made this the largest free festival in the UK.

Our number of Twitter followers grew to over 41,600.

Join them and keep up to date with what's new via @UniOfYork.

£43,405 was raised for student projects

via our crowdfunding platform YuStart.

49,927 hours were volunteered by our students.

That's over £300,000 worth of work!

286,156 books were borrowed from the library.
£500,000 was pledged for scholarships for refugees.

The scheme will provide financial support for three undergraduate students per year for three years.

4,248,031 hours were spent in classes.

That's a lot of learning - good work students and staff, you've earned your festive break!

And finally...

We also launched our International Pathway College, began work on a new education and training partnership for TV and film, and began building four fantastic new teaching and social spaces on campus.

Merry Christmas!

We wish all our students, staff, alumni and friends a relaxing festive break and all the best for 2016. Thanks for being part of it.
