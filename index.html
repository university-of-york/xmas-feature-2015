2015

The Year in Review

It's been a busy year at the University of York. New buildings have been built, graduands have become graduates and this year's new influx of students have descended on campus.

Scroll down to see some of the statistics that have made up 2015 for us.

Scroll down
1 new Chancellor

On 27 October we welcomed our sixth chancellor, Professor Sir Malcolm Grant.

A new 2nd in command

Professor Saul Tendler took over as Deputy Vice-Chancellor and Provost.

3 faculty boards

Our departments are now organised into three faculties: Arts and Humanities, Sciences and Social Sciences.

3.5 hours' wait in the final queue for the Willow Disco

The infamous nightclub closed its doors for the last time in July.

7 Research Champions

The Research Champions introduced our new research themes as part of the Chancellor's Inauguration.

10th in the UK for Research Council income

We secured £46m in research funding in 2014/15.

24 shows were performed in the Drama Barn
25th worldwide in Arts and Humanities

in the THE World University Rankings.

29 delegates from around the world pledged their support for the York Accord

supporting Higher Education institutions in war zones across the world.

42 PhD students received funds thanks to alumni donations
45 working days were spent clearing goose poo from paths around campus
Viewers from 51 countries watched graduation online
55th in the world for Life Sciences

in the THE World University Rankings.

60 scholarships for Masters study were awarded to students from under-represented groups

Each award was worth £10,000.

86 points lay between York and Lancaster as we won the the Carter James Trophy for the 26th time

The 51st Roses Tournament was kicked off by England Football Manager Roy Hodgson.

100 articles have been contributed to The Conversation by our academics
103rd in the QS World University Rankings

Up 17 places since last year!

121 undergraduate bursaries

were funded by donations from alumni and friends.

182 public lectures on campus

with almost 18,000 attendees.

242 of our students went abroad

with help from the Centre for Global Programmes.

251 visiting students came to York

from 28 countries around the world.

1,302 students joined the new Graduate Research School
1,803 books were added to the library

thanks to suggestions from students via the MoreBooks scheme.

1,916 new publications were added to the York Research Database by our academics
2,569 alumni donors made a gift to the University
3,376 modules were taught
5,585 students graduated

including 2,363 BAs and BScs, 1,630 Masters and 316 PhDs.

8,817 tickets were issued for Festival of Ideas events

This year's festival included over 150 free talks, performances and exhibitions.

12,778 plants were planted across campus
14,717 people booked to attend Open Days
15,353 students made up this years' student body
£43,405 was raised for student projects

via our crowdfunding platform YuStart.

49,927 hours were volunteered by our students

That's over £300,000 worth of work!

126,000 hours of projector use
286,156 books were loaned from the library
£500,000 was pledged for scholarships for refugees
£4,034,000 in philanthropic donations was given by our alumni and friends
4,248,031 hours of lectures, tutorials, practicals and seminars were timetabled for our students

Merry Christmas!

Phew, what a year! We're looking forward to 2016 being even bigger and better!
